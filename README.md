# Agentic AI in Healthcare: Supplementary Appendices

This repository provides supplementary materials for the article:

**A Systematic Review of Agentic AI in Healthcare: An Evidence-Informed Seven-Principle Framework**

The study synthesizes recent peer-reviewed empirical evidence on agentic AI in healthcare and organizes the evidence using an evidence-informed seven-principle framework. The framework integrates established concepts from agentic AI, clinical AI, and healthcare governance literature to assess deployment readiness across clinical decision support, patient monitoring/ICU, and administrative workflow settings.

## Abstract

Agentic artificial intelligence (AI) systems capable of autonomous goal-directed behavior, multi-step planning, tool use, multi-agent coordination, and iterative self-correction represent a shift from passive clinical AI tools toward systems that can participate in complex healthcare workflows. However, empirical evidence remains fragmented across clinical decision support, monitoring, and administrative applications, with limited synthesis of which agentic principles are technically demonstrated and which are sufficiently validated for responsible clinical deployment. This review synthesizes 25 peer-reviewed empirical studies using an evidence-informed seven-principle framework covering autonomy, planning, tool use, multi-agent collaboration, explainability, human oversight, and safety/compliance. The findings show that current evidence is concentrated in technical capability principles, while human oversight, safety/compliance, and equity-related evaluation remain underdeveloped.

## Transparency Note

These supplementary files are provided to support transparency, reproducibility, and auditability of the systematic review process. The seven-principle framework should be interpreted as an evidence-informed synthesis of existing agentic AI, clinical AI, and healthcare governance literature, not as a claim that the underlying principles are newly invented.
